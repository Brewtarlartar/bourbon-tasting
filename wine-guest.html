How to Taste

👀
See
Observe the color and clarity. Tilt against something white.
🌀
Swirl
Gently swirl to release aromas. Watch the "legs" on the glass.
👃
Smell
Dip your nose in. What do you notice first? Fruit? Flowers? Earth?
🍷
Sip
Small sip. Let it coat your whole mouth. Notice the finish.
"There are no wrong answers—trust what you taste."

Pinot Noir Red
Monterey, California
Did You Know? Called "the heartbreak grape" because it's so hard to grow. Its name means "black pinecone"—the tight clusters resemble pinecones. It's also Chardonnay's parent.
Nose
Red cherry, raspberry, earthy notes, dried herbs
Palate
Silky texture, bright red fruit, soft tannins, cola

Primitivo Red
Puglia, Italy
Did You Know? Primitivo is genetically identical to California's Zinfandel. Both trace back to a Croatian grape called Crljenak Kaštelanski. Same DNA, different continents!
Nose
Blackberry, plum jam, dried fig, black pepper, cocoa
Palate
Full-bodied, dark fruit, baking spices, velvety, warming
