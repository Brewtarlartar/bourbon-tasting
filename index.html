What Makes Bourbon "Bourbon"?

Legal Requirements

  • Made in the United States
  • Mash bill contains at least 51% corn
  • Aged in new, charred oak barrels
  • Distilled to no more than 160 proof
  • Bottled at 80 proof or higher
  • No added coloring or flavoring

Did You Know?

While 95% of bourbon is made in Kentucky, it can legally be produced anywhere in the United States.

The "new barrel" requirement means bourbon barrels can only be used once—they're then shipped worldwide for aging scotch, whiskey, and tequila.

The Art of Tasting

1. Visual

Observe color and clarity. Deeper amber suggests longer aging in the barrel.

2. Nose

Gently swirl and inhale. Start with the glass below your nose, gradually raising it closer.

3. Palate

Take a small sip and let it coat your entire mouth. Notice the flavors evolving.

4. Finish

After swallowing, breathe out gently. Note the lingering flavors and sensations.

Pro Tip: Adding a few drops of water or a small ice cube can "open up" the bourbon, releasing hidden aromas while reducing the alcohol burn.

High West 92 Proof

American Prairie Bourbon

Utah's first legal distillery since 1870, High West is a pioneer in the craft of blended American whiskeys.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Park City, Utah
  • Founded: 2007
  • Style: Blended Straight Bourbons
  • Aged: 2-13 years (blend)

Mash Bill

  • 75% Corn
  • 21% Rye
  • 4% Malted Barley

High West: The Story

Founded by biochemist-turned-distiller David Perkins, High West revived Utah's whiskey tradition with an innovative approach to blending.

2007 Utah's first legal distillery since Prohibition opens
Philosophy Blending as an art form, not a shortcut to production
Innovation Pioneered barrel-finished American whiskeys
Location World's only ski-in distillery and saloon

Giving Back: High West donates 10% of after-tax profits from American Prairie Bourbon to preserve and restore the prairie ecosystem.

High West: Tasting Notes

Nose

Light caramel, sweet vanilla, bright orange peel

Palate

Candy corn, honey nougat, sweet cornbread, subtle anise, orange zest

Finish

Vanilla with caramel apple and pecan pie notes—smooth and lingering

Overall: Approachable and sweet-forward with complex undertones. Perfect for both bourbon newcomers and enthusiasts.

Basil Hayden 80 Proof

Kentucky Straight Bourbon Whiskey

The lightest-bodied bourbon in Jim Beam's prestigious Small Batch Collection, known for its distinctive high-rye spiciness.

Quick Facts

  • Introduced: 1992
  • Created By: Booker Noe
  • Distillery: Jim Beam, Kentucky
  • Character: Spicy yet smooth

Mash Bill

  • 63% Corn
  • 27% Rye (High!)
  • 10% Malted Barley

The Legend: Basil Hayden Sr.

The brand honors Meredith Basil Hayden Sr., an 18th-century rye farmer who revolutionized bourbon making.

Late 1700s Hayden migrates from Maryland to the Kentucky frontier
1796 Changes his bourbon recipe to include higher rye content
Legacy Credited as the "Father of High Rye Bourbon"
Impact Created an entirely new genre of American whiskey
"A gentle introduction to high-rye bourbon's distinctive character."

Basil Hayden: Tasting Notes

Aroma

Charred oak dominates, with sweet vanilla and caramel, hints of dried fruit

Taste

Charred oak, sweet brown sugar, distinctive black pepper, dried fruit complexity

Finish

Pleasant, lingering charred oak with dried fruit—clean and peppery

Overall: Light-bodied with pronounced spice. The high rye creates a peppery kick that distinguishes it from sweeter bourbons.

Buffalo Trace 90 Proof

Kentucky Straight Bourbon Whiskey

From the world's most award-winning distillery, a bourbon with over 200 years of heritage and exceptional craftsmanship.

Quick Facts

  • Distillery Founded: 1775
  • Current Name Since: 1999
  • Location: Frankfort, Kentucky
  • Aged: Minimum 8 years

"The World's Most Award-Winning Distillery"

Buffalo Trace: A Historic Legacy

1775 Founded as Old Fire Copper (O.F.C.) Distillery by Hancock Lee
1886 Renamed George T. Stagg Distillery after new ownership
1904 Became Ancient Age Distillery under Schenley ownership
1999 Becomes Buffalo Trace Distillery

Why So Many Names?

Each name change reflected new ownership—common in an industry that survived Prohibition, the Great Depression, and countless mergers. The 1999 rebrand to "Buffalo Trace" honored the ancient buffalo migration path that crossed these grounds, connecting the distillery to Kentucky's frontier heritage.

Buffalo Trace: Tasting Notes

Appearance

Deep amber with brilliant clarity—evidence of extended aging

Palate

Rich smoky notes with light sweetness, caramel, vanilla, toffee, robust spicy character

Finish

Long and warming with lingering spice and oak—sophisticated and complex

Overall: Bold yet balanced, with layers of complexity. A bourbon that rewards contemplation and demands attention.

Maker's Mark 90 Proof

Kentucky Straight Bourbon Whisky

The bourbon that pioneered the premium category, distinguished by its soft red winter wheat and iconic hand-dipped wax seal.

Quick Facts

  • Founded: 1953 by Bill Samuels Sr.
  • Location: Loretto, Kentucky
  • Innovation: Wheat instead of rye
  • Aged: 6-7 years (to taste)

First distillery designated a National Historic Landmark while in active production (1980)

Maker's Mark: Family Heritage

Oct 1953 Bill Samuels Sr. purchases Burks' Distillery for $35,000
1954 Production begins with revolutionary wheat-based recipe
1958 First bottles released to market
Philosophy Create bourbon Bill himself would enjoy drinking
"It tastes expensive... and is." — 1960s-70s marketing tagline

The Revolutionary Wheat Recipe

Bill Samuels Sr. made a bold decision: reject traditional rye in favor of soft red winter wheat.

Why Wheat?

Traditional bourbon uses rye, creating spicy, peppery notes.

Wheat produces a softer, sweeter bourbon with front-of-palate sweetness—never bitter or sharp.

This revolutionary choice created the "wheated bourbon" category.

Iconic Details

  • Red Wax: Every bottle hand-dipped
  • Square Bottle: Premium cognac-style
  • "Whisky" Spelling: No "e"—honors Scottish heritage
  • Aged to Taste: Not by time, typically 6-7 years

Maker's Mark: Tasting Notes

Character

Smooth and balanced—never bitter or sharp

Sweetness

Front-of-palate sweetness from red winter wheat, immediately approachable

Flavors

Pleasant vanilla from barrel charring, soft caramel, hints of fruit, gentle oak

Overall: The ultimate sipping bourbon. Wheated softness makes it exceptionally smooth while maintaining complexity.
