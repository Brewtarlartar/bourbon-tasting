## BOURBON TERMINOLOGY CHEAT SHEET

**Small Batch** = Limited barrels blended together  
**Straight Bourbon** = Aged min. 2 years, no additives  
**Wheated Bourbon** = Uses wheat instead of rye (softer)  
**Age Statement** = Age of youngest bourbon in bottle  
**Char Level** = How deeply barrel is burned (affects flavor)  
**Finish** = Lingering flavors after swallowing  
**NAS** = No Age Statement (varies)  
**Cask Strength** = Not diluted before bottling (higher proof)

## EMERGENCY ANSWERS TO COMMON QUESTIONS

**"Which should I buy?"**  
‚Üí Start with what you liked tonight! Each has its purpose.

**"How do I store bourbon at home?"**  
‚Üí Store upright, away from sunlight. Doesn't age in bottle (unlike wine).

**"Is ice okay?"**  
‚Üí Absolutely! Try it both ways and find your preference.

**"Are expensive bourbons better?"**  
‚Üí Not always. Buffalo Trace is affordable and exceptional. Blind tastings often surprise people.

**"Can I visit these distilleries?"**  
‚Üí Yes! Most offer tours. Kentucky Bourbon Trail is amazing.

**"Why does bourbon taste like vanilla?"**  
‚Üí Charred oak barrels! Charring caramelizes wood sugars, creating vanilla compounds.

**"What's the difference between bourbon and whiskey?"**  
‚Üí All bourbon is whiskey, but not all whiskey is bourbon. Bourbon has specific legal requirements (51% corn, new barrels, made in USA).
