## Bourbon Basics (Slides 3-5) | 10 minutes

### Slide 3: What Makes Bourbon "Bourbon"?
**Key Teaching Moments:**
- Emphasize that bourbon is a **legally defined** spirit with specific requirements
- Fun fact: "Only 5% of bourbon is made outside Kentucky, but it can be made anywhere in the USA"
- The "new charred oak barrel" requirement is why bourbon barrels can only be used once (they then go to Scotland, Ireland, Mexico for scotch, whiskey, tequila aging)

**Engagement:**
- Ask: "Who thought bourbon had to be made in Kentucky?" (Most will raise hands)
- Explain the corn requirement: "That 51% corn is what gives bourbon its characteristic sweetness"

### Slide 4: The Art of Tasting
**Demonstrate Each Step:**
1. **Visual:** Hold glass up to light, swirl gently, observe color
2. **Nose:** Important technique - start with glass below nose, gradually raise it
   - "If you stick your nose right in the glass, the alcohol will overwhelm everything"
   - "Take short, gentle sniffs rather than one big inhale"
3. **Palate:** Take a small sip, let it coat your tongue
   - "Don't swallow immediatelyâ€”let it sit for a moment"
   - "Try to move it around your mouthâ€”different areas of your tongue detect different flavors"
4. **Finish:** After swallowing, breathe out gently through your nose
   - "Notice what flavors linger and how long they last"

**Pro Tip to Share:**
- "Adding water isn't 'cheating'â€”it's actually a professional technique. A few drops can open up the bourbon and release aromatics that were trapped by the alcohol."

### Slide 5: Pairing Principles
**Make It Relatable:**
- Compare to wine pairing (but note bourbon's higher alcohol means different rules)
- **Complement example:** "Sweet with sweetâ€”like honey and tea"
- **Contrast example:** "Salty pretzels with sweet caramel bourbonâ€”the contrast makes both more interesting"
- **Fat principle:** "Think of how a buttery croissant can tame even the spiciest food"
